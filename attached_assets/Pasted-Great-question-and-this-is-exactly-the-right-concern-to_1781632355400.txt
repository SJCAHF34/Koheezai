Great question — and this is exactly the right concern to have before you embed something like Koheez in Teams.
I’ll give you the real answer, not the marketing one:
👉 Nothing in Teams or the app itself “automatically prevents HIPAA violations.”
Compliance is entirely about how the app and your tenant are designed, governed, and contracted.
🔴 First — critical reality (don’t skip this)
No app is “HIPAA compliant” by default
Compliance depends on:
configuration
contracts (BAAs)
how data flows
how users behave [hipaajournal.com]
👉 So the question isn’t:
“What prevents PHI from leaking?”
👉 It’s:
“Where are the controls across the full data path?”
🧠 Think of it as a chain of responsibility
For your Koheez + Teams scenario, PHI flows like this:
User → Teams → Koheez (web app/API/backend) → Storage
HIPAA must be enforced at every step, NOT just Teams.
✅ 1. What protects PHI in Teams itself
Teams can support HIPAA, but only if configured.
Core controls you must enforce:
Identity & access
MFA + Conditional Access
RBAC / least privilege access
Session controls
👉 Prevents unauthorized access to PHI [accountablehq.com]
Data protection
Encryption (in transit + at rest)
Sensitivity labels
DLP policies to block PHI sharing
👉 Controls what users can see/share [accountablehq.com]
Governance
Audit logs
Retention policies
Monitoring and alerting
👉 Detects misuse or breaches
⚠️ 2. The biggest gap: Koheez (this is where risk is)
This is what most teams miss:
✅ Microsoft’s BAA does NOT cover Koheez
👉 You need a separate Business Associate Agreement with Koheez
Otherwise:
You are automatically out of compliance
Because:
Any SaaS handling PHI becomes a Business Associate
Must sign a BAA + meet HIPAA controls [accountablehq.com]
✅ 3. What Koheez MUST implement (non-negotiable)
If Koheez handles patient data, it must enforce:
🔐 Technical safeguards
Encryption (TLS + data at rest)
Secure APIs (OAuth / token-based auth)
Audit logging of all access
Strict access controls (RBAC)
👉 Required for HIPAA Security Rule [censinet.com]
🔎 Data handling controls
“Minimum necessary” access (role-based)
No PHI in logs / debug outputs
De-identification where possible
👉 Limits exposure of patient data [accountablehq.com]
📊 Operational controls
Risk assessments
Incident response (breach notification)
Staff training
Vendor security reviews
👉 Required administrative safeguards [accountablehq.com]
✅ 4. What you must control (shared responsibility)
This is the part people get burned on:
Microsoft secures the platform
YOU secure the data usage
Your responsibilities include:
Tenant / Teams controls
Block risky third-party apps
Restrict external sharing
Enforce DLP and retention
Integration governance (your specialty area)
Validate Koheez data flows
Control what the agent can send to Koheez
Prevent PHI from entering Copilot prompts unintentionally
App-level governance
Review Koheez architecture:
Where is data stored?
What logs are kept?
Who has access?
